Run Luke, run!

By Jonathan Carvalhosa, IME (Instituto Militar de Engenharia) Brazil

Timelimit: 1

The Death Star will explode and Luke Skywalker has to get out of there as fast as possible.

To hinder his escape, droids are positioned at certain points of the star.

The star map is a rectangle composed only by empty spaces, walls and droids.

The droids don`t move and can shoot at straight line along its row and its column, but their shots cannot pass through walls.

As Luke is disarmed, he can`t defend shots and need to escape without entering the line of sight of any droid.

You must create a program to check if Luke escapes the star or not.

Input

The first line of input contains two integers n and m (2 <= n, m <= 100), indicating the height and width of the Death Star.

The next N lines have m characters each.

Empty cells are represented by the character '.' , cells with droids are represented by the character 'D' and walls are represented by '#'.

Luke begins at the upper left cell, and the exit of the Death Star is the lower right cell.

The point at which Luke begins and the exit of the star always contains empty cells.

Output

The output of the problem should be a single line containing:

The string "Run Luke, run", if he can escape from the star.

The string "May the force be with you", if he can not escape.

Sample Input Sample Output

5 6

.#.D..

.#D...

..##..

#.....

D...#.

Run Luke, run
